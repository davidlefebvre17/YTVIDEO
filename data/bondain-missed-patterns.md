## 1. Earnings Season Patterns (NOT in knowledge files)

The existing files have no dedicated earnings-season knowledge. Bondain's transcripts are rich with recurring patterns about how results season shapes markets.

### 1.1 Post-Publication Asymmetry
- **Stock performance after results is NOT just about beating/missing consensus.** It integrates: comparables (YoY trajectory), the narrative quality of management guidance, market trend at time of publication, and a series of more or less rational circumstances.
- When valuations are stretched (generous PE multiples built on exceptional growth trajectory), any normalization of growth triggers outsized punishment. Stocks that were priced for perfection get "piétinées" (trampled) at the slightest miss.
- Multiple stocks can lose 15%+ in a single session after results, even without a dramatic miss, simply because expectations were too high.

### 1.2 "Réinitialisation des abus passés" (Reset of Past Excesses)
- Brutal post-earnings drops can be a healthy reset. Less complacency in the market is positive long-term.
- When growth was "extraordinary in the literal sense" and the trajectory normalizes, it is logical that things adjust downward.

### 1.3 Guidance > Results
- The forward-looking guidance matters more than backward-looking results. Companies that raise guidance get rewarded disproportionately; those that lower it get punished regardless of past-quarter results.
- Investors "font la chasse aux gagnants par ricochet" (hunt for indirect winners) early in a tech cycle, then pivot to "chasse aux perdants par ricochet" (hunting indirect losers) when the disruption narrative matures.

### 1.4 Cascading Sector Effects
- Results from sector bellwethers (e.g., a luxury conglomerate) set the tone for the entire sector. A weak result from one leader can drag down all peers regardless of their individual performance.
- "Portes-étendard sectorielles" (sector standard-bearers) like luxury houses, semiconductor firms, or industrial conglomerates define the mood for their whole vertical.

### 1.5 The Results + AI + Monetary Policy Triptych
- During certain periods, three forces simultaneously shape markets: (1) earnings season, (2) the AI narrative lottery, and (3) monetary policy expectations. The interaction between these three creates amplified volatility.

## 2. Sector Rotation & Small/Mid Cap Patterns

### 2.1 The Dow Jones / NASDAQ Divergence Signal
- When the Dow Jones outperforms the NASDAQ 100, it signals that "old economy" companies are taking their revenge on "new economy" ones. This configuration is a classic rotation signal.
- The podium of gains populated by healthcare, fast food, and banking names "fleurit bon l'Amérique du siècle précédent" (smells like last-century America).

### 2.2 Small Cap / Russell 2000 Rotation Mechanics
- Small caps (Russell 2000) become the rotation play when the Fed signals an accommodative pivot. Lower rates disproportionately benefit smaller companies with higher debt sensitivity.
- The small cap trade activates when investors "explore la thématique des petites valeurs dans l'hypothèse d'une Fed très accommodante" (explore small caps on the hypothesis of a very accommodative Fed).
- However, small cap rallies are often ephemeral and depend on rate cut expectations actually materializing.

### 2.3 Offensive-Defensive Duality
- Markets often simultaneously buy both offensive (tech/AI) and defensive (gold, utilities) assets. This "art d'être offensif-défensif" reflects unresolved investor tension between FOMO and survival instinct.
- When tech and gold rise together, it signals uncertainty, not conviction. The market is hedging both outcomes.

### 2.4 Cyclical Revenge
- Periods of increased volatility from trade tensions tend to trigger "le retour de la vengeance des actions cycliques" (the return of cyclical stock revenge). Sectors like energy, financials, and utilities benefit from rotation out of tech.
- Value investors get "goulées d'air frais" (gulps of fresh air) during these rotations.

## 3. Triple Witching / Options Expiry Patterns (NOT in knowledge files)

This is a significant gap. Bondain explains these mechanics pedagogically.

### 3.1 Witching Days Mechanics
- **Every 3rd Friday of each month** is a "journée des sorcières" (witching day) where certain options expire.
- **Every 3rd Friday of the 3rd month of each quarter** is a **triple witching day** (triple sorcières): three expirations telescope simultaneously: stock options, index options, and index futures.
- These represent enormous volumes (e.g., $5,500 billion in options expiring in a single session in the US).
- The concept dates from the 1980s at Wall Street.

### 3.2 Behavioral Effects
- Additional volatility comes from the "débouclages" (unwinding) of positions.
- The day before triple witching tends to see exaggerated moves as investors anticipate the unwinding.
- After extended winning streaks (e.g., 7 consecutive up days for the NASDAQ), witching day becomes the natural moment where investors "sifflent la fin de la récrée" (blow the whistle on recess).
- AI/tech stocks tend to be disproportionately affected: they can drop 3%+ on the witching day while value sectors rise (energy, utilities, financials).

### 3.3 Put Options as Hedging Signal
- When investors buy put options (options de vente) on specific stocks like major tech names, it signals hedging activity rather than outright bearish conviction.
- The existence of heavy put activity on a stock can create additional downward pressure during expiry.

## 4. Meme Stock / Short Squeeze Patterns (NOT in knowledge files)

### 4.1 Meme Stock Anatomy
- The pattern requires: (1) a nostalgic/iconic brand, (2) a charismatic social media leader, (3) a stock disconnected from fundamentals with extreme volatility, (4) heavy short interest creating squeeze potential.
- Retail investors "frénétiquement achètent ce qui avait fonctionné" (frenetically buy what worked before) -- a pattern of behavioral repetition.
- Gains can be extreme (74% for GameStop, 78% for AMC, 36% for Tupperware in a single session).

### 4.2 Short Squeeze Mechanics
- Short-selling funds must review their positions whenever a meme stock leader resurfaces, to avoid getting "explosé" (blown up).
- The ideal meme stock target: "une société qui a une marque ancienne et forte et qui serait en grande difficulté financière et qui ne vaudrait plus grand-chose en bourse" (an old, strong brand in severe financial difficulty trading for almost nothing).
- The Roaring Kitty pattern: social media post -> viral spread -> retail buying frenzy -> short squeeze -> massive volatility, all disconnected from fundamentals.

### 4.3 Contagion Effect
- When one meme stock activates, others follow (AMC, Tupperware, Hertz) even without being mentioned by the influencer. Retail investors buy the entire "meme basket" from memory.

## 5. Market Concentration / Magnificent Seven Patterns

### 5.1 Index Distortion from Heavy Weightings
- When the largest index weightings ("pondérations") move aggressively, they drag the entire index regardless of market breadth.
- A small number of mega-cap tech stocks can account for the majority of index movement, creating a misleading picture of overall market health.

### 5.2 AI Investment Cascade Model
- Phase 1: Buy "vendeurs de pelle" (pick-and-shovel sellers) -- semiconductor companies, infrastructure providers.
- Phase 2: Buy the large platforms with AI capabilities.
- Phase 3: Hunt for "gagnants par ricochet" (indirect winners) -- energy companies, commodity providers feeding AI data centers.
- Phase 4 (current shift): Hunt for "perdants par ricochet" (indirect losers) -- sectors AI may disrupt: insurance, legacy software, gaming, wealth advisors.
- Each phase creates its own market narrative and sector rotation.

### 5.3 The Two Unanswered Questions
- Market nervousness around AI-exposed stocks boils down to two questions: (1) Are the hundreds of billions invested in AI going to waste? (2) Who are the ultimate winners? Until these resolve, volatility persists.

## 7. Market Microstructure Patterns

### 7.1 Government Shutdown and Data Blackout
- When the US government shuts down, official economic statistics stop being published. This creates a "brouillard" (fog) where markets trade without their usual data anchors, increasing volatility from non-fundamental sources.

### 7.2 Absence of Catalysts
- Some of the most confusing market sessions come when there is no macro data, no major earnings, no policy decisions. Markets fall back on narratives and momentum, creating choppy, directionless price action.
- These periods are when small signals (a social media post, a minor geopolitical comment) get amplified beyond their fundamental significance.

## 8. Valuation Patterns

### 8.1 Exuberance That Is "Not So Irrational"
- Markets can enter a state of exuberance that initially appears irrational but may be fundamentally supported by: record earnings, rate cut expectations, and strong employment data all aligning.
- The challenge: when multiple tailwinds align, investors collectively believe "à tous les coups on gagne" (win every time), creating a complacency bubble even when individual factors are rational.

### 8.2 Performance Comparison Trap
- The widening gap between US and European market performance creates its own feedback loop: capital flows to the outperformer, further widening the gap.
- This creates eventual mean-reversion potential when the gap becomes extreme.

## 10. Trade War / Tariff Negotiation Pattern (Enrichment)

### 10.1 The Sinusoidal Pattern
- Trade negotiations between major powers follow a "sinusoïde" (sine wave): threat -> escalation -> market sell-off -> conciliation signal -> market recovery -> new threat.
- Markets "se font encore prendre" (still get caught) despite knowing the pattern, because the president "reste imprévisible dans sa prévisibilité" (remains unpredictable in his predictability).
- The doctrine: "Un bon bourpif avant une caresse" (a good punch before a caress).

### 10.2 The Bessent Factor
- Having a stabilizing figure (Treasury Secretary) acts as a market anchor. When the political leadership is volatile, markets look to the economic team for reassurance.
- "L'élément économique stable du régime" (the stable economic element of the regime) becomes a de facto market signal.
